R66 NxG Police

BUILT TO PROTECT & SERVE. Fully outfitted before delivery and ready for service immediately, the four seat R66 NxG Police Helicopter is designed to give law enforcement every advantage. Those advantages start with a powerful Rolls Royce RR300 Turbine engine. The R66 features all of the reliability, efficiency and cost-effectiveness that Robinson helicopters are known for — plus a state-of-the-art imaging system, advanced navigational equipment and up to three hours of flight time.

ABOUT THE R66 NxG POLICE

A force multiplier, right from the factory. Tough, rugged and ready, this overwatch workhorse optimizes fuel efficiency – allowing you to stay on station for up to three hours with standard fuel capacity. For agencies requiring even longer endurance, optional auxiliary fuel tanks are available, further extending mission duration and range.

  • Cost-Effective: One-third the acquisition cost and half the operating cost of comparable law enforcement helicopters.
  • Proactive Patrol: Enhanced cost-efficiency allows for more proactive patrolling and reduced reaction times to calls.
  • Maximized Airtime: Less ground time means more time in the air, providing greater community protection.
  • Advanced Technology: A modern and technologically sophisticated helicopter.
  • Reliable Parts Availability: Vertical integration at Robinson ensures consistent parts availability.

The aircraft comes mission-ready with the WESCAM MX-10, a compact, multi-sensor imaging system ideal for low-altitude tactical surveillance and patrol. The SX-7 Starsun searchlight and dual 6-channel audio controllers ensure your crew stays connected, aware, and in control—whether on patrol, tracking suspects, or supporting tactical teams.

Now equipped with an all-glass cockpit, the R66 NxG features advanced digital displays for enhanced situational awareness and reduced pilot workload. Engine and system instruments are integrated into the Garmin TXi flight display, streamlining critical information and providing a modern, intuitive interface.

Rugged, reliable, and easy to maintain, the R66 NxG features a proven two-blade rotor system that reduces hangar space requirements. An impact-resistant windshield provides critical protection against bird strikes, and foreign object damage, enhancing crew safety and keeping the aircraft mission-ready in challenging environments.

Rolls-Royce RR300 Turbine Engine

The Rolls Royce RR300 turbine engine is a modern, lightweight turboshaft engine designed primarily for use in light helicopters. The Rolls Royce RR300 turbine engine combines advanced technology, proven design principles, and a focus on reliability and efficiency, making it a popular choice for light helicopter applications.

Hard Point on Belly

A hardpoint is a location on the R66’s belly designed to carry an external load.

Steps to Access Rotor Head

To facilitate reaching the main rotor hub, steps are built into the three cowl doors on the left side of the cabin.

Engine Oil Filter
Engine Air Filter
Main Gearbox Oil Filter
160 Amp Starter/Generator

Used for engine starting and 160 AMP electrical power generation.  A single push of the start button latches the starter on until 58% N1.

Electronic Engine Monitoring Unit

The EMU is a digital recording device that continuously monitors and records N1, N2, engine torque and MGT. EMU status can be checked by pressing the Test Button on the annunciator panel.

Engine Anti-Ice System

User selectable system that allows hot compressor discharge air to flow thru the front compressor support to keep ice from forming.

Engine Compressor Rinse Nozzle

A nozzle that is built into the engine filter housing to make engine gas path washes and rinses easier to accomplish.

Lithium-ion Battery

The TB17 battery weighs 16 lb, which is significantly lighter than the R66’s standard battery (52 lb). Lithium-ion chemistry holds a higher charge voltage and provides a more constant discharge rate than lead-acid, delivering consistent, more reliable turbine starts. The battery’s internal heater enhances functionality in cold temperatures as low as -40°C.

Annunciator Light Panel

The R66 features an Annunciator Panel located at the top of the instrument panel and consists of illuminated segments. If a caution or warning condition occurs, the appropriate segment illuminates to indicate the nature of the problem. A test button is located to the right of the annunciator panel that allows the pilot to test the panel prior to flight.

RPM Governor

The RPM Governor monitors the engine's RPM and makes adjustments to keep the engine's RPM constant.

Cockpit Video Camera, 4K Ultra HD

The 4K Ultra HD (3840 x 2160 pixels) video camera records video, intercom audio, radio communications, and GPS position to a removable 128 GB USB flash drive. Recording starts automatically when the helicopter is turned on and stops when it is turned off.  Recording video and/or audio to the flash drive can be independently controlled with separate switches.

Leather Seats
Aft Center Console

The Aft Center Console replaces the center rear seat and provides secure storage.

Clear Windshield and Door Windows
Adjustable Pedals on Pilot’s Side

The R66 helicopter is equipped with adjustable tail rotor pedals on the pilot's side, allowing the pilot to adjust the pedal position for comfort and optimal control. Adjusting the pedals is easy with a quick-release pin on each side of the pedal, allowing the pedal to be moved to the desired position.

Pulsing LED Taxi/Landing Lights
Panel, Task, and Overhead Lighting

Panel, Task, and Overhead Lighting provide illumination for instruments and consulting navigation charts, and flight aids. They also provide lighting for the rear seating area.

LED Anti-Collision Light and Navigation Lights

The R66 features LED Anti-Collision and Navigation Lights to improve the visibility of the helicopter in the air and on the ground.

Garmin GFC 600H Autopilot

The Garmin GFC 600H autopilot integrates seamlessly with Garmin flight displays and offers features like altitude hold, heading select, navigation, and approach modes to increase both ease of operation and safety. Additional safety functions include low-G and low-altitude protection, limit cueing, and hover assist. The system reduces pilot workload with features like airspeed stabilization and optional yaw axis control.

Air Conditioning

The simple, lightweight system provides air-conditioned comfort and reduced humidity throughout the four-seat cabin. An overhead console provides vents for each seat with 250 CFM total airflow. The 42 lb air conditioner uses the latest automotive refrigerant and has 17,000 BTU/hr cooling capacity. The system is controlled by a toggle switch with low and high fan settings and uses approximately three horsepower during operation. The evaporator and fan are mounted under the aft center seat, preserving all four under-seat baggage compartments. The compressor engages when the fan is switched on and automatically disengages during autorotation entry to maximize glide performance.

Hydraulically Boosted Controls

The Hydraulically Boosted Controls on the R66 assist the pilot in controlling the helicopter's flight surfaces via the cyclic and collective.

Crashworthy Fuel System

The R66 features a Crashworthy Fuel System with a Bladder Tank designed to prevent fuel leakage and reduce the risk of fire in the event of a crash. The system incorporates several features and design elements to enhance safety for both the helicopter and its occupants. The R66's fuel tank is constructed of an aluminum enclosure containing a flexible, puncture- and tear-resistant bladder that can withstand significant impact without rupturing. Additionally, the fuel system includes flexible joints or self-sealing breakaway couplings that absorb energy and prevent breakage. These are designed to disconnect safely and seal off the fuel line if excessive force is applied, preventing fuel spillage.

Impact-Resistant Windshield

Impact-resistant windshields for R66 helicopters provide protection from bird strikes. Impact-resistant windshields are constructed of tough, energy-absorbing polycarbonate (standard windshields are made of acrylic) and installed with distinctive retention hardware. A special hard coating provides protection from scratching and weather degradation. Tests demonstrate the R66 windshields provide protection for an impact with 2.2 lb (1 kg) bird at speeds up to 100 knots. Note: Polycarbonate windshields, as compared to standard acrylic windshields, will have more minor surface or optical imperfections.

Cabin Heater and Defogger

The Cabin Heater and Defogger outlets are located near the floor on both sides of the instrument panel forward of the tail rotor pedals. Warm air is bled off the engine compressor and routed through tubes to the forward outlets and to the rear footwells. A heater control knob is located to the left of the cyclic stick.

Removable Dual Controls

Robinson's center-positioned cyclic makes it easy to enter and exit the cockpit. It’s very effective for dual instruction, as well as sharing piloting duties.

Fuel-Flow Meter

The fuel flow meter provides real-time fuel flow data to the GTN which in turn displays the fuel consumption rate along with fuel range rings on a moving map. Fuel used, fuel remaining, fuel to destination, and other real- time information is also available on accessory pages.

External Power Receptacle

A 28-volt SAEAS35061-style receptacle is provided for external power. The receptacle is located in the cabin outboard of the pilot’s seat.

Glass Instrument Panel

The all-glass cockpit features Garmin’s advanced digital displays (GDU 700P/1060) and is designed to enhance situational awareness and ease pilot workload. Engine and system data are fully integrated into the Garmin TXi flight display, delivering streamlined access to critical information through a modern, intuitive interface.

Rotor Brake

The helicopter’s Rotor Brake allows the pilot to stop the rotor quickly, reducing shutdown time and risk of injury to ground personnel and passengers.

Vertical Card Compass
Low Rotor RPM Light and Horn

The Low Rotor RPM Light and Horn indicate rotor RPM is below safe limits.

High Rotor RPM Audio Alert

The R66 features a High Rotor RPM Audio Alert that emits a warble tone in the headsets.

Door Opener, Gas Spring, All Four Doors
High Engine Torque/MGT Audio Alert

This alert is equivalent to the First Limit Indicator found in some larger turbine helicopters. It warns the pilot when nearing operating limit without the need to continuously monitor multiple engine instruments.

Three-Point Shoulder Harnesses
Carpeting and Acoustic Interior
Under-Seat Storage Compartments

Under-Seat Storage Compartments under each seat allows for stowing soft carry-on bags, briefcases, and personal belongings.

Door Locks
