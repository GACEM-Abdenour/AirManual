Contact the Parole Board of Canada

Notice:

Do not transmit personal and sensitive information by e-mail.

Find out how to contact the Parole Board of Canada (PBC).

By topic:

Offenders

To request or submit offender-related information or questions (e.g. support letter, request to observe a parole hearing, etc.), contact the PBC regional office nearest to you.

Record Suspensions / Pardons

To request information related to record suspensions / pardons and to get help with your application, contact:

  • Information line: 1-800-874-2652 (toll-free)
  • Email: suspension@pbc-clcc.gc.ca
  • Regular mail:

Clemency and Record Suspension Division
Parole Board of Canada
410 Laurier Avenue West, 5th Floor
Ottawa, Ontario 
K1A 0R1

For more information, visit Record Suspensions.

Expungement

To request information related to expungement and to get help with your application, contact:

  • Information line: 1-800-874-2652 (toll-free) - 8:00 a.m. - 6:00 p.m. (EST), Monday-Friday
  • Email: expungement-radiation@pbc-clcc.gc.ca
  • Regular Mail:

Parole Board of Canada
Clemency and Record Suspension Division
410 Laurier Avenue West
Ottawa, Ontario 
K1A 0R1

For more information, visit Expungement.

Victim Services

To register as a victim, or to submit or request information:

  • Call the Victim Information Line at 1-866-789-4636 (Toll-free) to be automatically connected to a Regional Communications Officer (RCO) in your region for assistance with and information on PBC victim services; or
  • Contact the PBC regional office nearest to you.
  • Visit the Victims Portal at: https://victimsportal-portailvictimes.csc-scc.gc.ca/Main/Home

For more information, visit Victims and the parole process.

Observing a Hearing

To observe a PBC parole hearing:

  • complete the Request to Observe a Parole Board of Canada Hearing - Application form, and once completed send it to the PBC regional office where the hearing will take place or the office nearest to you.
Decision Registry

To request a copy of a PBC decision, complete the Decision Registry Request Form. Once completed, send the form to the PBC regional office serving the area where the offender is imprisoned or under supervision or, if not known, the office nearest to you.

For more information, visit Decision Registry.

PBC Outreach Program

The Parole Board of Canada (PBC) conducts regular outreach across Canada to increase awareness and understanding of its mandate, programs and services. To learn more about the PBC’s Outreach Program and/or request an outreach session, visit our Outreach page.

Access to Information or Privacy Request

To make an Access to Information or Privacy Request, consult How to Make an Access to Information Request or How to Make a Personal Information Request.

Media Enquiries 

The Parole Board of Canada (PBC) is committed to providing media with information on its policies and operations in a timely and transparent manner. Visit our Media page for contact information.

Accessibility

The PBC welcomes feedback on the accessibility of its programs and services. Comments or suggestions can be sent to the PBC’s Designated Official for Accessibility through the feedback form or through alternate ways listed below. All feedback may be submitted anonymously.

If you would like to be contacted regarding your feedback or concern(s), please provide your contact information (optional).



Alternate ways to contact the PBC about accessibility:

  • Email: Accessibility.Accessibilite@pbc-clcc.gc.ca
  • Voicemail: 1-833-541-3063 (toll-free)
  • Teletypewriter (TTY): (613) 954-7771
  • Mail:  Parole Board of Canada
               Designated Official for Accessibility
               410 Laurier Avenue West, 6th floor mailroom
               Ottawa, Ontario
               K1A 0R1

Feedback received will be catalogued and included in the PBC's annual progress reports without any identifying details. To find out more about this process, read the PBC Feedback Process.

General Enquiries

For general enquiries, email info@pbc-clcc.gc.ca or contact your nearest PBC regional office.

Note: the Correctional Service of Canada (CSC) is responsible for administering federal sentences of two years or more, managing correctional institutions, and supervising offenders under conditional release in the community. To contact the CSC, visit: www.csc-scc.gc.ca/contact-us/index-eng.shtml.

By geographical location:

Important: Please send all offender-related enquiries and documentation, including letters of support, to the PBC regional office where the offender is located.
