Ranks and appointment

Ranks in the Canadian Forces mark a person's position in its hierarchical structure. As people gain more responsibility and authority, they earn promotions in rank.

The formal rank structure within the Forces is essential for:

  • passing orders in operations
  • ensuring clarity of command
  • maintaining order and discipline

Insignia, the badges worn on uniforms, show the person's rank. You can see the insignia for each rank at:

  • Royal Canadian Navy ranks and badges
    • The RCN has instituted a gender neutral nomenclature change in the Junior Rank designations: This rank change will be reflected upon amendment of QR&O 3.01
      • Master Seaman to Master Sailor (MS)
      • Leading Seaman to Sailor 1st Class (S1)
      • Able Seaman to Sailor 2nd Class (S2)
      • Ordinary Seaman to Sailor 3rd Class (S3)
  • Canadian Army ranks and badges
  • Royal Canadian Air Force ranks and badges
  • Canadian Special Operations Forces Command (CANSOFCOM) ranks and badges
