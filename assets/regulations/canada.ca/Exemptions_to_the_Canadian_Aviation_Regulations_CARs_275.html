Applicant provision(s): MHI RJ Aviation ULC

Aircraft model: n/a

Registration no.: n/a

QC-007-2025- re-issuance of QC-007-2020

This exemption enables MHI RJ Aviation ULC. to maintain, in accordance with section 561.03 of the CARs, a Manufacturer Certificate when the company does not meet all the elements required to sign a statement of conformity as required by CARs paragraph 561.10(1)(d), all the elements of a production control system required by CARs subsection 561.08(1) nor a final assembly facility, as required by CARs subsections 561.03(8) and by Standards for Approved Manufacturers subsections 561.08(a), 561.08(b) in part, and 561.08(e).

This exemption, subject to the conditions set herein, also allows MHI RJ Aviation ULC, or persons acting under the control of MHI RJ Aviation ULC, to sign a statement of conformity in respect of an aeronautical product where the aeronautical product has not been manufactured as required by CARs paragraph 561.10 (1)(d).

This exemption extends the regulatory relief previously granted under QC-007-2020.

NCR-012-2025

The purpose of this exemption is to allow persons to conduct a special aviation event, and suitably qualified pilots-in-command to perform flyovers, at funerals, holiday ceremonies, celebrations, weddings, and sporting events without having a Special Flight Operations Certificate-Special Aviation Event (SFOC).

Applicant provision(s): Global

NCR-039-2025

The purpose of this exemption is to increase the validity period of synthetic flight training equipment certificates issued pursuant to subsection 606.03(2) of the CARs as follows:

  • A certificate issued pursuant to subsection 606.03 (2) remains in force where the synthetic flight training equipment in respect of which the certificate has been issued is re-evaluated:
    • in the case of a flight simulator, at least every 12 months; or
    • in the case of a flight training device, at least every 24 months.
  • In the case of a flight simulator, the certificate shall remain in force until the first day of the thirteenth (13) month,
  • In the case of a flight training device, the certificate shall remain in force until the first day of the twenty-fifth (25) month.

Transport Canada will undertake additional oversight activities as necessary, within the re-evaluation intervals specified above, in accordance with its oversight responsibilities.

NCR-028-2023

The purpose of this exemption is to enable a regulatory sandbox in which Transport Canada will develop and test a regulatory innovation to better support the ‘approved data’ needs of Canadian aircraft modification service providers, aircraft owners and aircraft operators. The regulatory sandbox will create an environment in which Transport Canada can safely experiment with a proposed new method for aircraft certification delegates to approve engineering technical data in support of certain eligible major modifications without the need for a supplemental type certificate (STC). The regulatory sandbox will help to generate evidence or information about how this innovative process could interact with and impact people, existing markets, and regulation in the real world.
