Key features

Stay informed wherever you are: Get current conditions and 7-day forecasts for more than 10,000 locations across Canada, including your current and saved locations.

Track air quality easily: Check your local Air Quality Health Index (AQHI) levels with just a tap.

Get weather alerts fast: Receive push notifications for severe weather, air quality updates, and extreme temperatures.

See the weather clearly: Use interactive radar features to zoom in on high-resolution radar animations, track precipitation types like rain, snow and hail by intensity, and view real-time lightning strikes across Canada. Learn more about interactive radar features.

Use it your way: Enjoy features that suit your preferences, such as dark mode, a home screen widget, and full support in English and French.

Learn something new: Discover weather facts and climate tips in the message centre. Note: message centre notifications cannot be turned off.

Set up custom air quality notifications

Set up your own custom notifications for air quality in one or multiple locations.

  1. Open the WeatherCAN app.
  2. Tap on the ‘More’ tab in the lower right-hand corner of the display.
  3. Tap “Notifications”. 
  4. Tap “Air Quality”. 
    • AQHI information is only available for locations where data can be collected. 
    • If your location does not have air quality data, you can set notifications for a nearby area.
    • You can also contact your local public health office, Ministry of Environment, or local lung and asthma association chapter for existing local information on air quality and your health.  
  5. Select “Enable AQHI Notifications”.
  6. To pick the same AQHI level at all saved locations: choose “Same Level for All Locations” and select your desired level. 
  7. To pick different AQHI levels at different locations: tap on each location name separately and choose the level you want.

What you need to know about air quality notifications

What is the Air Quality Health Index?

The Air Quality Health Index or "AQHI" is a scale designed to help you understand what the air quality around you means to your health. The AQHI is measured on a scale ranging from 1-10+. Learn more about the Air Quality Health Index. 

When do AQHI notifications happen?

Once you have set up your notifications for a specific location and level of air quality, you will be notified when the air quality, compared to the previous hour:   

  • reaches your chosen AQHI level or higher
  • goes below your chosen AQHI level after reaching it earlier

Choosing the right AQHI level for alerts

  • You should choose your AQHI level using your own experience and symptoms as a guide  
  • If you are sensitive to air pollution, you may want to set notifications for the moderate risk category (4 to 6) 
  • If you are not sensitive to pollution, you may only want to set notifications for when AQHI reaches the high-risk category (7 to 10)

How often do you get AQHI notifications?

AQHI conditions are updated every hour. The number of AQHI notifications you get will depend on: 

  • how many locations you choose 
  • the AQHI level you choose for each location 

How can I limit the number of alerts?

  • turn off notifications for more than one location 
  • increase your chosen threshold of AQHI 

Custom AQHI notifications are not air quality advisories

You can set your AQHI notifications for any value between 3 and 10+.  

However, Special Air Quality Advisories only go out when there is a high level of health risk to the general population (when the AQHI reaches 7).   

As a result, if you or someone in your care is sensitive to air pollution, it can be helpful to set AQHI alerts for lower levels, and to take precautions before the AQHI is too high.

Set up custom temperature notifications

Set up your own custom notifications for high and low temperatures, humidex and wind chill. You can set notifications for multiple locations at the same thresholds.

These custom notifications alert you when the observed temperature reaches, or the forecast temperature is expected to reach, your chosen value(s) so you can make informed decisions using up to date data. 

  1. Open the WeatherCAN app. 
  2. Tap on the ‘More’ tab in the lower right-hand corner of the display.
  3. Tap “Notifications”.
  4. Tap “Temperature”.
  5. Set the “Temperature Notifications” to On.  
  6. Set the temperature, humidex, and/or wind chill from -60°C to 45°C by scrolling through the values and tapping on the desired value(s). These values will be applicable for all locations that you have turned on temperature notifications. Individual thresholds for each location cannot be set due to technical limitations.
  7. Select “All Locations” to receive notifications for your current location and all saved locations. Alternatively, select individual locations by setting them to On if you only want to receive notifications for certain sites. 

How to leave feedback

You can leave feedback directly in the app. Tap the "More" button in the bottom navigation, go to the "About us" section and select "Feedback".

If you're not able to leave feedback in the app, use the Contact us page. Select the Problem or feedback button, then choose WeatherCAN app from the dropdown menu.
