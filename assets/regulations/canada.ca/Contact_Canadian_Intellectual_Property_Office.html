Frequently asked questions

  • How do I register a copyright?

    In Canada, copyright exists automatically when an original work or other subject matter is created, provided the conditions set out in the Copyright Act have been met.

    Registration is not required for protection, but a certificate of registration is evidence that it exists and that the person registered is the owner. The request can be sent online, by mail or by fax. To register your copyright online:

    Register a copyright

    CIPO has a wide variety of educational content to help you learn more about IP, including e-learning modules, fact sheets, infringement information, international resources and more!

  • Can I receive official electronic correspondence for my trademark?

    Yes! In March 2023, the Trademarks Office and the Trademarks Opposition Board launched the Trademarks Document Retrieval Service (TDRS) in order to send Office correspondence electronically.

    How to change your method of correspondence:

    How to retrieve your trademark documents online:
