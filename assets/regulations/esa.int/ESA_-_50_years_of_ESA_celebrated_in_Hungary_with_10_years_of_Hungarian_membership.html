50 years of ESA celebrated in Hungary with 10 years of Hungarian membership

21/10/2025 239 views 0 likes
ESA / About Us / 50 years of ESA

At the Infótér annual Space and Defence Day in Balatonfüred in Hungary, the European Space Agency co-organised an event celebrating jointly the 50th anniversary of ESA and 10 years of successful membership of Hungary in ESA.

The panel was composed of representatives of government Szabolcs  Szolnoki, (Deputy State Secretary, Head of Hungary's Delegation to ESA Council), János Lichtenberger (science), János Solymosi (industry), Réka Szabó (Women In Aerospace) and Andrea Vena (ESA). 
Kai-Uwe Schrogl (ESA) and Peter Kristóf Co-Chair - Infotér Space and Defence Day, who organised the conference to the highest standards, co-moderated the panel, which touched on Hungary’s experiences in benefits from and future expectations regarding its ESA membership.

The panel ended with the presentation of the 50th Anniversary Statement to the Head of Hungary’s ESA delegation, signed by ESA Member States on 18 June 2025 in Paris. The panel showed the positive assessment of all stakeholders of Hungary’s engagement in ESA and a promising future of mutual benefits, where Hungary brings into the European framework a dynamic and creative national space sector.

During the conference, the agency representatives attended a panel organised by WIA-Europe Hungary Regional Network and WIA-Europe Slovakia Regional Network. Panellists were Dr Zsófia Bodó, Lenka Tkácová and Hanga Katreiner, moderated by Réka Szabó. The panel explored how we can build a stronger, more inclusive space and defence sector, by rethinking what resilience and sovereignty mean for nations, organisations and individuals, and exploring how mindset, mentorship and diversity can make our communities truly future-ready. 

In the plenary section of the conference, ESA representatives Andrea Vena and Kai-Uwe Schrogl were invited to give keynotes on sustainability in the space sector and, respectively, on current issues in space law and regulations.

The conference day was rich in substance and exchanges. It is not only the flagship platform for Hungary but also is an effective forum, establishing partnerships - also between the space industry associations - in the region.
