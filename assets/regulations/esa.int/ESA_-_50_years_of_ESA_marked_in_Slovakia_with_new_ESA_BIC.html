50 years of ESA marked in Slovakia with new ESA BIC

12/12/2025 115 views 0 likes
ESA / About Us / 50 years of ESA

The most recent national celebration of 50 years of the European Space Agency took place in Slovakia, marking a significant milestone in the country’s rapidly developing role within the European space ecosystem. The event was held in connection with the official opening of the ESA Business Incubation Centre (BIC) Slovakia in Košice.

This achievement is closely aligned with the country’s 'Space Strategy 2030', published in 2024, and the entry into force of Slovakia’s first national space law in 2025. Together, these steps highlight a strong and coherent national commitment to industrial development and innovation, positioning Slovakia as one of Europe’s promising new 'space tigers'. The establishment of ESA BIC Slovakia is another important building block in this trajectory of success.

ESA was represented at the opening ceremony by Nicolas Helssen, ESA’s Project Manager for ESA BIC Slovakia, who joined with the representatives of the host institution, the Technical University of Košice (TUKE). The event was held in the presence of TUKE’s Rector, underlining the strong link between the incubation centre and the academic and research communities in the region.

To celebrate ESA’s 50th anniversary, a dedicated panel discussion was organised in cooperation with the delegation, bringing together leaders from across Slovakia’s space ecosystem. The session was co-moderated by Kai-Uwe Schrogl from ESA side and Marek Kosuda, TUKE Vice-Dean for Development and the dynamic driving force behind the establishment of the BIC.

The panel featured contributions from:

  • Kristína Jacková – Ministry representative
  • Michal Brichta – Head of the Slovak Space Office
  • Šimon Mackoviak – Representative of the scientific community
  • Maria Čopková – Representative of the Slovak space industry

Their discussion highlighted Slovakia’s growing capabilities and the maturation of its institutional framework. The participants shared the views on the country’s relationship and cooperation with ESA and looked ahead to Slovakia’s expectations and aspirations within the agency.
